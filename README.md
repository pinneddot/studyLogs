### I. Explore Identity and Access Management
 
**What is IAM?**
  A system and its main job is to make sure the right people have the right access to the right resources at the right time.<br/>  
  `Authorization` - is the process of confirming that an identity is who they claim to be by their credentials.  
  `Authentication` - determines the resources and actions that an identity is permitted to access.  
  `SSO` - allows users to authenticate to multiple applications and services using a single set of credentials, without having to reenter them each time.  
  `Federated identity (FIM)` - While SSO authenticates a single credential across various systems within a single organization, FIM enables single sign-on to multiple applications across multiple enterprises. It establishes a trusted relationship using digital certificates and signatures.  
  `Identity governance and administration (IGA)` - is a security framework for managing and auditing resource access throughout the identity lifecycle.
  `User lifecycle management (LCM)` - sets the foundation. It is the process of managing user accounts and access rights within an organization, from onboarding to offboarding.  
  `Privileged access management (PAM)` - secures the infrastructure. PAM focuses on mitigating threat risks by managing and monitoring access to your critical systems and data.  It enforces the principle of zero trust and least-privileged access to reduce the attack surface.  
  `Identity Threat Protection with Okta AI` - provides continuous protection against identity threats.
